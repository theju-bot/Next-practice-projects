---
title: 'The Singing Fish of Batticaloa Lagoon'
date: '2024-03-05'
---

One of Sri Lanka's most mysterious natural phenomena happens quietly beneath the waters of **Batticaloa Lagoon**. On certain nights, locals claim to hear a faint musical humming rising from the water — a sound so consistent it earned the town its nickname: **"Land of the Singing Fish."**

The lagoon stretches over 56 kilometres along the eastern coast, separated from the Indian Ocean by a narrow strip of land. It is home to:

- Dozens of fish species unique to the eastern coast
- Migratory birds that arrive between October and March
- Traditional fishing communities who have worked its waters for generations

Scientists have proposed several theories — molluscs vibrating against rocks, specific fish species producing mating calls — but **no definitive explanation** has been agreed upon.

What is certain is that the lagoon is the lifeblood of Batticaloa. Fishermen set out before dawn in small wooden crafts called *vallam*, casting nets by memory and instinct passed down through families.

Whether or not the fish truly sing, the lagoon holds a kind of quiet magic that is difficult to explain to someone who has not sat at its edge on a still night.
